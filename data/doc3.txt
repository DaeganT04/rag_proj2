Work-for-Hire Acknowledgement, Confidentiality, and
Noncompetition Agreement
For and in consideration of new employment by Software Solutions
Integrated, LLC (the “Company”), the signer (“Employee”) agrees as
follows:
I. Ownership of Developments and Rights
All programming, documentation, software design, ideas, concepts,
techniques, inventions, processes, and works of authorship, plus all
associated or related copyrights, patents, trade secrets, and other
intellectual property rights (collectively, the “Work Product”) developed or
created by Employee while performing work for Company or its customers
shall belong exclusively to Company and shall be considered works for
hire of the Company under U.S. copyright law. To the extent Work
Product may not be considered work for hire for Company, Employee
agrees to assign, and automatically assigns to Company at the time of
creation of the Work Product, without any requirement for further
consideration, all right, title, and interest Employee has in the Work
Product. Upon request by Company, Employee shall take such further
actions, including without limitation signing and delivering instruments of
conveyance and any other documents, that Company may require to give
effect to this assignment.
II. Adequacy of Compensation
Employee’s normal salary is full payment for all Work Product, including
without limitation programming, documentation, and software design,
developed or created for Company or its customers by Employee during
the period of Employee’s employment by Company, and Employee has no
claim for royalties or other compensation, other than Employee’s normal
salary, for such Work Product.
III. Trade Secrets
Employee acknowledges that Company considers all information of a
competitively sensitive or proprietary nature, including without limitation
software design, development, documentation, and other Work Product
(collectively, the “Confidential Information”) to be trade secrets belonging
to Company. Confidential Information does not include information that
Employee can show (1) is generally available to the public, (2) has been
released by Company or its customers generally without restrictions, (3)
has been independently developed or acquired by Employee without
reliance in any way on other protected information of Company or its
customers, or (4) has been approved in writing by Company or its
customers for use and disclosure by Employee without restriction. Both
during the term of Employee’s employment by Company and after
termination of that employment for any reason, Employee will not use,
divulge, or discuss with third parties any Confidential Information learned,
received, or otherwise acquired by Employee during such employment
without prior written permission from Company.
IV. Restrictions on Competition
Because of Employee’s familiarity with Confidential Information pertaining
to software for agricultural business management and agricultural
accounting, Employee could cause grave harm to Company if Employee
worked for a competitor of Company anywhere in the U.S. Hence, AFTER
TERMINATION OF EMPLOYEE’S EMPLOYMENT, EMPLOYEE WILL
NOT COMPETE WITH COMPANY FOR TWENTY-FOUR MONTHS BY
ENGAGING IN THE DESIGN, DEVELOPMENT, OR DOCUMENTATION
OF SOFTWARE FOR AGRICULTURAL BUSINESS MANAGEMENT OR
AGRICULTURAL ACCOUNTING IN THE U.S. Employee will not perform
such activities or directly manage or supervise them as a principal, agent,
employee, consultant, contractor, co-venturer, or in any other capacity.
V. Miscellaneous
This Agreement merges and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous
agreements, undertakings, covenants, and conditions (including
specifically all prior confidentiality and noncompetition agreements
Employee has entered), whether oral or written, express or implied,
between Employee and Company to the extent they contradict or conflict
with the provisions of this Agreement.
Although Employee’s employment is contingent on the acceptance and
observance of this Agreement, this Agreement shall not make Employee’s
employment other than terminable at will at any time by Employee or
Company in the sole discretion of either of them.
